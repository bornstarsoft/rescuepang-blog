In a recent internal memo, Asha Sharma, the new head of Xbox, revealed that the Game Pass subscription service has become "too expensive" for players, hinting at upcoming changes to its pricing structure. This announcement comes just six months after Microsoft raised the price of its top-tier Game Pass by a staggering 50%, now costing $30 per month. Sharma emphasized the need for a "better value equation," indicating that the current model may not be sustainable in the long run.

The Game Pass service, which offers various tiers including Ultimate, Premium, Essential, and PC Game Pass, has been a cornerstone of Xbox's strategy to provide value to gamers. However, with the rising costs and the inclusion of blockbuster titles like Call of Duty, Microsoft has faced significant revenue losses, reportedly giving up over $300 million in sales last year. This situation raises questions about the future of such high-profile games on the platform.

As Microsoft navigates these challenges, it will be interesting to see how they adapt Game Pass to meet both player expectations and business needs. Will they find a balance that keeps gamers satisfied while ensuring profitability? Only time will tell.
