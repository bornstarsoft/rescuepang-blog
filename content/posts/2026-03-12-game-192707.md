The recent release of Slide Viking: Treasures Path has left many fans of the original Slide Viking feeling disillusioned. While the first game was a refreshing take on sliding puzzles, the sequel takes a sharp turn into sokoban territory, leaving behind the innovative mechanics that made its predecessor a hit. This shift in genre has sparked disappointment among players who were expecting more of the same sliding puzzle goodness.

Despite its shortcomings in originality, Slide Viking: Treasures Path does offer a polished sokoban experience. Afil Games has crafted clever and challenging levels, complete with quality pixel art and a user-friendly interface. However, for those who cherished the unique elements of the first game, this sequel feels like a missed opportunity to build on its predecessors success.

As we reflect on this transition from a sliding puzzle to a crate-pushing game, one can't help but wonder: what does the future hold for the Slide Viking franchise? Will Afil Games return to their roots, or are we destined for more spin-offs that stray from the original's charm?
